# Handoff Packaging

Turn verified requirements and supporting business context into a downstream-ready handoff package without drifting into implementation planning.

## Quick start
1) Read the verified requirements and supporting source artifacts.
2) Summarize the business problem and desired outcome.
3) Build traceability from objectives and process needs to the requirements.
4) Capture risks, dependencies, unresolved questions, and next decision points.
5) Populate or strengthen the local handoff artifact.

## Workflow

### 1) Read the handoff inputs
- Start with the local requirements specification artifact.
- Reference the business objectives and process analysis artifacts as needed for traceability and context.
- Pull in additional notes only when they materially improve the handoff.

### 2) Summarize the handoff
- State what is being handed off and why.
- Describe the business problem and desired outcome in terms a downstream reader can understand without redoing discovery.

### 3) Package verified requirements
- Summarize the requirements that are ready for downstream use.
- Keep speculative items, assumptions, and unresolved questions separate from the verified set.

### 4) Build traceability and context
- Map requirements back to:
  - business objectives
  - process needs
  - major business rules
- Record risks, dependencies, constraints, and external conditions that downstream roles need to know.

### 5) Capture unresolved decisions
- Include assumptions and open questions still in play.
- Describe the next decision points without turning them into implementation tasks.
- If the system includes AI-enabled or agentic behavior, separate:
  - business objectives
  - acceptance expectations for agent outputs
  - human oversight expectations
  - AI or agent-specific constraints

### 6) Produce the handoff artifact
- Populate or improve the local handoff artifact so downstream roles can continue without reconstructing the business context.

## Notes

- This skill is for handoff packaging, not for planning implementation.
- It should produce a clean downstream-ready artifact, not a task list or delivery plan.
- It is generic and reusable, but it is especially useful in the Business Analyst requirements handoff workflow.
